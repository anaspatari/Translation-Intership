- The Department for International Relations is available for people interested in partnerships with HEI, foreign organizations and institutions

As an intern with the office, I had to translate two study programme handbooks, which will be used by the foreign students interested in studying at Alecu Russo Balti State University. I had to select only those contents, which include course's name, USARB code, department, area, description, learning outcomes, contents, teaching and learning methods, assessment methods and recommended reading. The last day I received another document (an article) for translation. My task was to translate it from English into Romanian.


I am extremely grateful because of the lessons and skills I learned and the opportunities I had to apply these skills.


To begin with, I have improved my time-management skills. I received two texts (written in Romanian) for translation on. It was pretty difficult to manage time, especially when I was working from home and having a huge deadline (up to 24 of May). Although I split my working hours using an improvised calendar, my working schedule differed from day to day. Sometimes, I worked 15 minutes per day, sometimes - the whole night. Then I tried a different takt, which was much more useful for me. I broke a large task down (two big texts) into more manageable pieces (small texts). As a result, I translated at least 3-4 course syllabi every day. The work was much more productive because a small task was not associated with negative emotions. The last document was quite easy, as I had to translate it into my mother tongue. The limited deadline did not scare me. Moreover, it motivated me to work faster and more efficiently.

Although I mostly used google translate, I also learned to work with other online CAT tools, such as SMARTCAT, which is often used professionally. Smartcat is a cloud-based translation and localization platform that connects businesses, translators, and translation agencies in a single “Connected Translation” delivery loop. The platform positions itself as an “all-in-one” translation platform, combining CAT, TMS, and other translation technologies. I liked it very much because of the fast speed, which saves time. However, machine translation has its own disadvantages. It can translate only cliched texts. I had always to make such transformation as portioning, integration, antonymic translation etc. by myself.


As a consequence, I improved the ability of establishing translation difficulties and using the right method of translation.

The critical issue I have met translating from Romanian into English, was the translation of long sentences. I always tried to use, as much as possible, only short sentences in order to avoid mistakes. And the translation of academic text (characterized by long sentences) was a real challenge for me.


In English compound sentences, main sentences are the main parts, which are usually placed at the beginning of the sentences. That is to say, English sentences usually use the position of front center. This "from one to many" western way of thinking tends to focus on the beginning of a sentence. In other words, in English, the conclusion is described first, and the facts are described at the end of the sentence.


Example 1 (Romanian into English):

"Odată cu dezvoltarea tehnicii de calcul a apărut necesitatea studierii tehnologiilor informaţionale și comunicaționale la diverse nivele ale activităţii umane, inclusiv şi în instituţiile de învăţământ atât preuniversitare, cât şi universitare."


"There is a need to study information and communication technologies at various levels of human activity, including in both pre-university and university educational institutions because of the development of computer technology."

Example 2 (English into Romanian):

"Similar items were collated for comparison and contrast, and axial coding used to explore key themes."


"Pentru comparație și contrast, au fost adunate articole similare, iar pentru a explora teme cheie a fost folosită codificarea axială."


For the translation of English long sentences, the first thing is to carefully analyze the syntactic structure, an accurate understanding of sentence meaning, then adopt flexible translation methods for translation. The method of translation I have mostly used here was word order change (in order to preserve the aesthetic value of the original text and bring better reading experience to the readers as much as possible).


Another problem I have met translating from Romanian into English, was the the failure of understanding the contextual meaning in the source text. More specifically, I had to concretize the meaning of each term, in order to avoid translation errors.
